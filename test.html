Real-device scan validation

  1. Heart rate — open #/scan on a phone, cover the rear camera + flash with a fingertip, hold still in good light. Compare the reading to a pulse oximeter or a 15-second manual radial-pulse count (×4). Expect ±5–8 bpm at rest; then use “Compare to a real device” to store your personal offset.
  2. SpO₂ — compare to a real fingertip pulse oximeter. The camera estimate is uncalibrated — treat large gaps as “signal too weak”, not a true reading.
  3. Respiration — count breaths for 30 s and ×2. Stay still; talking or motion breaks the slow signal.
  4. Skin tone fairness — repeat the HR scan across a range of skin tones and lighting. Log where the signal-quality drops; camera-PPG is physically weaker on darker skin and in low light.
  5. Reaction — run #/reactionscan rested vs. tired; median should rise when fatigued. It’s processing-speed, not a medical test.
  6. Record results in a sheet (device, reference value, VITA value, delta, lighting, skin tone) to build a real accuracy picture before any clinical claim.
Reminder (hard rule): never label camera-vitals or AI triage as clinical/diagnostic — wellness/informational only. Do not publish a symptom-checker accuracy %.
